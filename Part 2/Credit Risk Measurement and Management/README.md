## Readings

- Sylvain Bouteille and Diane Coogan-Pushner, The Handbook of Credit Risk Management: Originating, Assessing, and Managing Credit Exposures, 2nd Edition (Hoboken, NJ: John Wiley & Sons, 2022).
	- Chapter 1. Fundamentals of Credit Risk 
		- Define credit risk and explain how it arises using examples.
		- Explain the differences between insolvency, default, and bankruptcy.
		- Identify and describe transactions that generate credit risk.
		- Describe the entities that are exposed to credit risk and explain circumstances under which exposure occurs.
		- Discuss the motivations for managing or taking on credit risk.
	- Chapter 2. Governance 
- Hennie van Greuning and Sonja Brajovic Bratanovic, Analyzing Banking Risk, 4th Edition (World Bank Group, 2020)
	- Chapter 7. Credit Risk Management
		- Describe key elements of an effective lending or financing policy
		- Explain the importance and challenges of setting exposure and concentration limits
		- Describe the scope and allocation processes of a bank’s credit facility and explain bank-specific policies and actions to reduce credit risk
		- Discuss factors considered during the credit asset classification process
		- Describe and explain loan loss provisions and loan loss reserves
		- Identify and explain the components of expected loss and differentiate between expected and unexpected loss
		- Explain the requirements for estimating expected loss under IFRS 9
		- Describe a workout procedure for loss assets and compare retaining versus writing off loss assets
		- Explain the components of credit risk analysis
		- Explain the components of credit risk management capacity and identify key questions for a bank’s board of directors
- Gerhard Schroeck, Risk Management and Value Creation in Financial Institutions (New York, NY: John Wiley & Sons, 2002).
	- Chapter 5. Capital Structure in Banks (pp. 170–186) 
		- Evaluate a bank’s economic capital relative to its credit risk
		- Identify factors used to calculate economic capital: probability of default, exposure, and loss rate
		- Define and calculate expected loss (EL)
		- Define and calculate unexpected loss (UL)
		- Estimate variance of default probability assuming a binomial distribution
		- Calculate UL for a credit portfolio and asset-level UL contributions
		- Describe how economic capital is derived
		- Explain how the credit loss distribution is modeled
		- Describe challenges in quantifying credit risk
- Michalis Doumpos, Christos Lemonakis, Dimitrios Niklis, and Constantin Zopounidis, Analytical Techniques in the Assessment of Credit Risk: An Overview of Methodologies and Applications (Springer, 2019)
	- Chapter 1. Introduction to Credit Risk Modeling and Assessment 
		- Explain the CAMEL system for evaluating banks
		- Describe quantitative credit risk measures: PD, LGD, EAD, EL, and time horizon
		- Estimate risk-weighted assets and capital adequacy ratios
		- Describe judgmental, empirical, and financial default prediction models
		- Apply the Merton model and describe its limitations
		- Compare Merton-based, CreditRisk+, CreditMetrics, and Moody’s-KMV approaches
		- Apply RAROC to measure loan performance
	- Chapter 2. Credit Scoring and Rating
		- Compare credit scoring systems and credit rating systems
		- Differentiate through-the-cycle and point-in-time rating systems
		- Describe development of credit scoring and rating models
		- Describe rating agency methodologies and criticisms
- Michel Crouhy, Dan Galai, and Robert Mark, The Essentials of Risk Management, 2nd Edition (New York, NY: McGraw-Hill, 2014).
	- Chapter 9. Credit Scoring and Retail Credit Risk Management
		- Analyze credit and other risks in retail banking
		- Explain differences between retail and corporate credit risk
		- Discuss the dark side of retail credit risk and mitigation measures
		- Describe credit scoring model types, variables, and applications
		- Discuss key mortgage credit assessment variables
		- Describe scorecard performance measures: CAP and accuracy ratio (AR)
		- Describe the customer relationship cycle and profitability trade-offs
		- Discuss benefits of risk-based pricing
